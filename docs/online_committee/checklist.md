## Online Committee Checklist

### General
    • Recruit an Online Committee Co-chair and 1–2 additional team members early during the planning cycle.
    • Attend weekly/bi-weekly organizer check-ins and report on online committee progress.
    • Document decisions and processes so future committees can build on them.

### Pre-Conference
    • Select an online conference platform in collaboration with the DEFNA board (previous platforms include Venueless; selection may be influenced by sponsorship deals and community needs).
    • Prepare or update the [Online Volunteer/Moderator Guidebook](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1OjwaufQv2jvXTQf4apjFkjqu9Cznj8QtW1o4URZ-Kdc/edit?tab=t.0#heading=h.iokuakmruk4m), covering platform navigation, moderation duties, Code of Conduct enforcement, and escalation paths.
    • Recruit online moderators/volunteers (coordinate with the Volunteer Committee for sign-up forms and onboarding).

### At-Conference
    • Brief and coordinate online moderators each morning before sessions begin and during emergencies.
    • Monitor the online platform throughout the day; respond to attendee questions and technical issues.
    • Actively engage with the online audience in chat where possible by welcoming attendees, highlighting talks, and fostering discussion.
    • Communicate in real-time with the A/V Chair to resolve any streaming or platform issues.
    • Escalate Code of Conduct concerns to the CoC team promptly.

### Other / Post-Conference
    • Collect feedback from online attendees and moderators on the platform and experience.
    • Write a post-conference retrospective (report) documenting what worked, what didn’t, and suggestions for the next year’s Online Committee.
    • Update the Online Volunteer Guidebook with lessons learned.
    • Coordinate with the A/V Chair on making recorded session videos available on YouTube after the conference.
