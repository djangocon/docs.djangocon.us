## Sprints Checklist

This checklist is written for the 2026 conference which is being held at the end of August. The dates should be updated for the next conference. 

### Pre-conference

- [ ] Brainstorm ways to make sprints friendlier to new contributors
- [ ] Update documentation dates
- [ ] Revise sprints questions on tickets
- [ ] Update the sprints page on the site

#### April

- [ ] Check-in about learning sprints after OG window closes

#### May and after

- [ ] Blog article on contributing to sprints
- [ ] Blog article on how to prepare for sprints as a leader
- [ ] Blog article on how to prepare for sprints as a contributor
- [ ] Social media posts on projects that will be showing up
- [ ] Social media posts on the sprints blog posts

#### June

- [ ] Check-in on venue
    - [ ] Confirm room with adequate space for 100 people
    - [ ] Ask about TV to support presentations
    - [ ] Confirm sprint supplies have been ordered (paper, markers, pens)
    - [ ] Confirm sprinters will have access to water
    - [ ] Ask about additional smaller room for new feature review team  
- [ ] Determine support needs for sprint leaders (in-person and virtual)
- [ ] Give or find someone willing to give lightning talk plugging sprints
- [ ] Find someone to lead intro to contributing to Django
- [ ] Find someone to lead the new feature review process
    - Go through the feature list together, air different views, and work toward consensus
    - Leave comments capturing what was discussed and where things landed
    - Consider pre-work to identify the order of features to review

#### July

- [ ] Reach out to sprint leaders with preparation resources
    - Project sprint leaders
    - Intro to contributing to Django speaker
    - New feature review leader 
- [ ] Reach out to sprint contributors with preparation resources

#### August

- [ ] Hold office-hour events to help people configure their machines
- [ ] Finalize plan for signs, pens, and markers

---

### Recruiting sprint leaders

- [ ] Recruit sprint mentors
    - [ ] Review list of ticket purchases for who is sprinting
- [ ] Encourage projects to have a list of easy tickets ready and publish them beforehand
- [ ] Post on social media asking who is going to sprints to lead a project
- [ ] Some projects to reach out to:
    - [ ] Django
    - [ ] PyBee
    - [ ] Django Girls
    - [ ] PyLadies
    - [ ] Django REST Framework
    - [ ] Wagtail
    - [ ] Other DRF packages
    - [ ] Django Commons (and its packages)
- [ ] Coordinate online sprint participation
    - [ ] Identify sprint project leaders who won't be in person (recruit, but don't actively plan until confirmed)
    - [ ] Set up Slack channels per project; encourage use of huddles for synchronous conversations
    - [ ] Coordinate with [Django Discord](https://chat.djangoproject.com)
    - [ ] Use the forum for project and sprint communication

---

### During conference

- [ ] Verify strong Wi-Fi in the venue (work with the venue team on this)
- [ ] Check the temperature of the sprints rooms
- [ ] Make sure sprinters know when & where lunch is so food isn't wasted
